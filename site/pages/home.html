Respiratory infection biology

Mapping host–pathogen interactions in space

We combine human samples, experimental infection models and spatial and single-cell technologies to understand pulmonary immune responses to emerging bacterial pathogens.

Our mission

From molecular states to infected tissue ecosystems

Pulmonary disease emerges from interactions among bacterial physiology, tissue microenvironments and host immunity. We resolve these processes within intact tissue and connect spatial signatures to disease mechanisms, biomarkers and therapeutic opportunities.

Spatial biology

Dual spatial host–pathogen transcriptomics

Disease biology

Approaches

Technology development and biological application

The platform links tissue pathology, bacterial biology, experimental models and computational analysis.

01

Spatial transcriptomics

FFPE and fresh tissue profiling, custom bacterial probes and pathology-guided analysis.

02

Single-cell RNA sequencing

Cell-state discovery in blood, airway and lung samples across infection phenotypes.

03

Experimental infection models

Air–liquid interface cultures and chronic pulmonary infection models.

04

Computational integration

Cross-platform validation, spatial neighborhoods and host–pathogen network analysis.
